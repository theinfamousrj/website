Stupid Numbers

So, as per the usual, I was chatting with my buddy the other day about numbers and just dumb stuff you'd imagine that two high teenagers talk about to sound deep. But he said something that bugged me and I couldn't let it go until I figured out what the fuck he was saying and if it was true. He said:

The set of all countable numbers is ridiculously small compared to the set of all real numbers.
My gut reaction was "WHAT THE HELL MAN?" My heart and brain were in why-would-you-do-this-to-me mode, I mean, it was close to 1 am and I did want to sleep at some point... So of course I wrote myself a note to go and see if I could somehow prove this to myself, and I did. But in proving it to myself I found another really neat concept called computable numbers which is even flipping weirder - but also kind of similar.

Countable Numbers

First and foremost, here's a wikipedia explanation of Countable sets. When determining if a set is countable we actually have 3 potential outcomes:

  • Countable
  • Countably Infinite
  • Uncountable
So I won't use goofy words like cardinality and instead I'm going to try to explain this more naturally. If we have a set (a list) of whole numbers that goes from 0 to infinity (∞) we call this the list of natural numbers and it is considered Countably Infinite. The length (cardinality) of this list is called aleph null (aleph zero or aleph nought) but we're just going to call it BIG. So make a list of numbers, count that, then think of the list of natural numbers. Is your list smaller? Then it's considered countable. Is it the same size as the list of natural numbers? Still countable but just countably infinite. Is it bigger than the list of natural numbers? Sorry, un-friggin-countable.
